Report Type
An abandoned vehicle is a vehicle that is out of tax and meets one of the following:
  • Damaged e.g. flat tires, broken windows, dents, extensive rust, broken lights etc.
  • Shows evidence that it has not moved for at least a month e.g. dirt, leaves, bird droppings covering vehicle etc.
A dangerous vehicle is a vehicle that may constitute a fire or health hazard on the estate by virtue of its condition, lack of care or potential accessibility. It does not necessarily need to be untaxed or parked without authorisation. A nuisance vehicle is a vehicle that is not dangerous, appears to have been abandoned but is in good condition and/or possibly of high value (Â£1,000 +) or a classic or collectable car.

Vehicle and Notice
Please attach at least four date stamped images on the date the notice was applied. Images should meet the following criteria:

  1. Close up of the fixed Refuse Disposal Notice on the windscreen with details fully legible.
  2. Front of the vehicle showing the fixed Refuse Disposal Notice and the vehicle number plate.
  3. The whole vehicle and immediate surroundings.
  4. The interior of the vehicle showing evidence of any property or rubbish in the vehicle.

Find vehicle make using one of these third-party websites: My Car Check | RAC Car Data Check
Please list what makes the vehicle appear abandoned; Missing wheels, flat tyres, smashed windows, damage to bodywork, evidence of not having been moved in some time, any rubbish inside the vehicle, road tax expired.
Vehicles parked on Islington Estates are not required to display a tax disc, if a vehicle is in good condition and does not have a tax disc on display you should use GOV.UK’s vehicle enquiry service
All suspected abandoned vehicles must have a Refuse Disposal Notice affixed to the windscreen, except burnt out vehicles which should be approached within health and safety guidelines.
